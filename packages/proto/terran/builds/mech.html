Mech Build

Matchup: TvZ, TvT

Difficulty: Advanced

A methodical, defensive playstyle focusing on mechanical units like Siege Tanks, Thors, and Hellions. This build excels in late-game scenarios and controlling ground-based armies.

Unit Composition

Core Army

  • Siege Tanks: 40-50% of army supply (main damage dealers)
  • Hellions/Hellbats: 25-30% (anti-light, meat shield)
  • Thors: 15-20% (anti-air, heavy damage)
  • Vikings: 10-15% (air superiority)

Support Units

  • Ravens: Detection and utility
  • Cyclones: Mobility and harassment
  • Banshees: Harassment (if going air)

Execution Guide

Early Game (0-8 minutes)

  • Focus on economy and tech structures
  • Build defensive Siege Tanks
  • Use Hellions for harassment and scouting
  • Avoid major engagements
  • Secure your natural expansion

Mid Game (8-15 minutes)

  • Build up Tank count (8-12 Tanks)
  • Add Thors for anti-air
  • Begin slow pushes with Tank lines
  • Control key map areas
  • Secure third base

Late Game (15+ minutes)

  • Massive army with 15+ Tanks
  • Control multiple fronts
  • Use Ravens for support abilities
  • Slowly advance across the map
  • Focus on positioning over speed

Key Strategic Points

  • Patience: Mech is about slow, methodical play
  • Positioning: Tank positioning is everything
  • Map Control: Control key areas with Tank lines
  • Economy: Focus heavily on economic development
  • Upgrades: Mechanical upgrades are crucial

Tank Positioning

Siege Tank Fundamentals

  • Siege Lines: Create overlapping fields of fire
  • High Ground: Always prefer elevated positions
  • Spacing: Avoid clumping against splash damage
  • Leap-Frog: Advance one line while other covers
  • Defensive Arcs: Cover all approach angles

Advanced Tank Tactics

  • Tank Drops: Use Medivacs to position Tanks
  • Multi-pronged Siege: Attack from multiple angles
  • Defensive Concaves: Spread Tanks in arc formation
  • Forward Positioning: Push Tanks closer during battles

Matchup Specific Strategy

vs Zerg

  • Early: Hellions to deny creep spread
  • Mid: Tank/Thor vs ground armies
  • Late: Add Vikings vs Brood Lords
  • Key Points: Deny Zerg expansions, control creep
  • Threats: Mutalisks, Brood Lords, Lurkers

vs Terran

  • Early: Tank up defensively
  • Mid: Tank vs Tank positioning battles
  • Late: Add air units for support
  • Key Points: Tank positioning, Viking control
  • Threats: Bio drops, air units, Tank pushes

Common Mistakes

  • Poor Tank positioning and spacing
  • Not building enough production facilities
  • Trying to move too quickly across the map
  • Inadequate anti-air preparation
  • Not upgrading mechanical weapons/armor
  • Overextending without proper support
  • Neglecting economic development

Upgrade Priority

  1. Siege Mode: Essential Tank upgrade
  2. Blue Flame: Hellion damage vs light
  3. Vehicle Weapons +1: Increase Tank damage
  4. Vehicle Plating +1: Tank survivability
  5. Drilling Claws: If using Widow Mines
  6. 250mm Strike Cannons: Thor vs massive
  7. Smart Servos: Thor transformation

Economic Management

  • Base Saturation: Keep all bases fully saturated
  • Gas Management: Mech is very gas-heavy
  • Expansion Timing: Expand behind defensive lines
  • MULE Usage: Constant MULE production
  • Supply Management: Don't get supply blocked

Advanced Techniques

  • Tank Drops: Use Medivacs for Tank positioning
  • Raven Support: Auto-Turrets and Point Defense Drone
  • Hellion Runbys: Harassment while main army sieges
  • Thor Transformation: Switch modes based on threats
  • Defensive Matrixes: Use to protect key units

Dealing with Counters

vs Air Harassment

  • Build Thors and Vikings early
  • Use Ravens for detection
  • Position anti-air defensively
  • Don't chase - stay defensive

vs Mobile Armies

  • Control choke points
  • Use Hellions for map control
  • Force engagements on your terms
  • Don't over-pursue fleeing enemies

vs Economic Pressure

  • Secure multiple mining bases
  • Use Tank lines to protect expansions
  • Counter-attack when enemy over-commits
  • Focus on cost-efficient trades

Transition Options

  • Sky Terran: Add Battlecruisers and Ravens
  • Bio Support: Add Marines for anti-air
  • Heavy Mech: Focus on Thors and Tanks only
  • Turtle Mech: Defensive play with Planetary Fortresses

Practice Drills

  • Practice Tank positioning on different maps
  • Work on leap-frog Tank advances
  • Practice Hellion harassment micro
  • Learn optimal production ratios
  • Practice defending vs different army compositions
