ZvZ Speedling Rush

Matchup: Zerg vs Zerg

Difficulty: Beginner

This aggressive early game build focuses on overwhelming your opponent with fast zerglings before they can establish proper defenses. Essential for ZvZ matchups and learning early game aggression.

Build Order

  1. 9 Overlord
  2. 12 Spawning Pool
  3. 11 Extractor
  4. 13 Overlord
  5. @Pool: 6 Zerglings, Queen
  6. @Pool: Metabolic Boost (Speed)
  7. 18 Hatchery (if defending)
  8. 19 Overlord
  9. Continue Zergling production
  10. Pull drones off gas after speed starts

Execution Guide

Opening (0-3 minutes)

  • Scout at 9 supply with your Overlord
  • Look for opponent's pool timing
  • Drone to 11, then build Extractor
  • Don't over-drone - need larvae for units
  • Assess opponent's defensive preparations

Mid Game (3-5 minutes)

  • Start speed upgrade immediately when pool finishes
  • Produce 6 zerglings right away
  • Pull 2 drones off gas after speed starts
  • Send zerglings to opponent's base
  • Continue larvae management for units

Attack Phase (4-6 minutes)

  • Attack when speed finishes
  • Target drones first, then buildings
  • Use speed to surround enemy units
  • Continue reinforcing with more zerglings
  • Maintain constant pressure

Key Strategic Points

  • Scout Information: Check opponent's pool timing and drone count
  • Gas Management: Only mine gas for speed, then pull off
  • Larvae Management: Save larvae for zerglings, not drones
  • Attack Timing: Hit as soon as speed finishes
  • Micro: Surround enemy units and workers

Scouting Information

What to Look For

Enemy Build Assessment

  • Pool First: Standard defensive build
  • Hatch First: Economic build - exploit with aggression
  • Gas Timing: Early gas = speed coming
  • Drone Count: Too many drones = vulnerable

Response Decisions

  • If opponent pools first: Match their timing
  • If opponent goes hatch first: All-in with speed
  • If opponent has early gas: Expect speed wars
  • Drone heavy opponents: Prime targets

Micro Techniques

Zergling Control

Basic Micro

  • Surround: Use speed to get around enemy units
  • Focus Fire: Target single units to reduce DPS
  • Retreat: Pull damaged units back
  • Reinforcement: Rally new units to the fight

Target Priority

  • Workers: Always prioritize drones
  • Queen: Important defensive unit
  • Spawning Pool: Prevent unit production
  • Hatchery: Economic damage

Defending Against Speedling Rush

Defensive Strategies

  • Match Pool Timing: Don't fall behind on military
  • Spine Crawlers: Build if needed for defense
  • Queen Usage: Use Queens for defense
  • Don't Over-drone: vs early pool builds
  • Wall Off: Use buildings if possible

Counter-Micro

  • Drone Pulls: Pull drones to fight if necessary
  • Queen Positioning: Use Queen range advantage
  • Spine Support: Fight near defensive structures
  • Economic Damage: Minimize worker losses

Transition Options

If Rush Succeeds

Follow-up Strategies

  • Continue pressure with more zerglings
  • Expand behind the attack
  • Tech to Banelings for follow-up
  • Maintain economic advantage

If Rush Fails

  • Retreat and build hatchery
  • Focus on economy recovery
  • Prepare for opponent's counter-attack
  • Tech to Roaches or Banelings

Advanced Tips and Techniques

Execution Refinements

  • Overlord Spotting: Use to spot opponent's army movement
  • Home Defense: Build spine crawlers if over-committing
  • Baneling Follow-up: Consider nest for continued pressure
  • Micro Practice: Practice zergling micro vs AI
  • Read Defense: Learn to assess defensive setups

Economic Balance

  • Drone Count: Stop at ~11-13 drones for attack
  • Gas Management: Mine exactly enough for speed upgrade
  • Supply Management: Build Overlords to avoid blocks
  • Recovery Planning: Prepare economic comeback if needed

Common Mistakes

  • Building too many drones early
  • Not scouting opponent's build properly
  • Attacking before speed finishes
  • Poor micro during engagements
  • Not following up with expansion if rush fails
  • Over-committing against heavy defense
  • Missing larvae injects during attack
  • Not adapting to opponent's defensive response

Map Considerations

Favorable Maps

Good Terrain

  • Short rush distances
  • Open attack paths
  • Limited defensive positions
  • Multiple approach angles

Difficult Maps

  • Long rush distances
  • Narrow choke points
  • High ground advantages
  • Easy wall-off positions

Build Variations

10 Pool Speedling

  • Purpose: Even earlier aggression
  • Trade-off: Less economic development
  • Use: vs hatch first builds
  • Risk: Falls behind if unsuccessful

12 Pool into Speed

  • Purpose: Slightly safer approach
  • Trade-off: Later attack timing
  • Use: vs defensive opponents
  • Benefit: Better economy backup

Psychology and Mind Games

Mental Warfare

  • Constant Pressure: Keep opponent on defensive
  • Fake Retreats: Bait opponent into poor positions
  • Economic Threats: Force defensive investments
  • Adaptation: Change tactics based on opponent's response

When to Use This Build

Ideal Situations

Perfect Targets

  • Players with poor early defense
  • Opponents who over-drone

Avoid Against

  • Early pool defensive builds
  • Players with good micro
  • Heavy spine crawler defense
  • Opponents who scout well
