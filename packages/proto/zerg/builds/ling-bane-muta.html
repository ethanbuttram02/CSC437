Ling/Bane/Muta

Matchup: ZvT, ZvZ

Difficulty: Advanced

An extremely mobile army composition combining Zerglings, Banelings, and Mutalisks for multi-pronged attacks and superior harassment. This build excels at overwhelming opponents through speed and multitasking.

Build Order

  1. 9 Overlord
  2. 15 Hatchery
  3. 16 Spawning Pool
  4. 17 Extractor
  5. 18 Overlord
  6. @Pool: Queen, 4 Zerglings
  7. 22 Baneling Nest
  8. @Natural: Queen
  9. @100 gas: Metabolic Boost
  10. 26 Lair
  11. 28 Extractor x2
  12. @Lair: Spire
  13. @Spire: Mutalisk production
  14. Continue Ling/Bane/Muta production

Unit Composition

Army Ratios

Ground Army

  • Zerglings: 60-70% of ground army
  • Banelings: 30-40% of ground army
  • Focus on speed and mobility
  • Cost-efficient trading units

Air Component

  • Mutalisks: 8-12 for harassment
  • Roughly 25% of total army supply
  • Harassment and map control focus
  • Hit-and-run specialists

Execution Guide

Early Game (0-8 minutes)

  • Standard opening with fast expansion
  • Get Baneling Nest for early pressure/defense
  • Research speed immediately
  • Scout opponent's tech choices
  • Apply early Ling/Bane pressure if possible

Tech Phase (8-12 minutes)

  • Get Lair as soon as possible
  • Add 2 more gas geysers
  • Build Spire when Lair completes
  • Continue Ling/Bane production
  • Prepare for first Mutalisks

Harassment Phase (12+ minutes)

  • Begin Mutalisk harassment
  • Use Ling/Bane for ground pressure
  • Multi-task between harassment and main army
  • Control multiple fronts simultaneously
  • Force opponent to split defenses

Key Strategic Points

  • Mobility: All units are extremely fast
  • Multi-tasking: Attack from multiple angles
  • Harassment: Constant economic pressure
  • Map Control: Superior speed for positioning
  • Cost Efficiency: Trade efficiently with speed

Mutalisk Usage and Micro

Harassment Targets

Target Priority

  • Workers (primary economic damage)
  • Tech buildings (delay progress)
  • Production (disrupt unit creation)
  • Supply/Overlords (create blocks)

Micro Techniques

  • Stacking for speed and protection
  • Magic Boxing to reduce splash
  • Hit and run tactics
  • Bounce damage vs clumped units

Zergling/Baneling Ground Army

Baneling Usage

Primary Targets

  • Marines (devastating damage)
  • Zerglings in ZvZ matchups
  • Buildings for structural damage
  • Clumped light units

Tactical Usage

  • Landmines for ambush attacks
  • Manual detonation timing
  • Flanking maneuvers
  • Escort with Zerglings

Zergling Support Role

  • Surrounds: Use speed to encircle enemies
  • Runbys: Economic harassment
  • Meat Shield: Protect Banelings
  • Cleanup: Finish damaged units

Matchup Specific Strategy

vs Terran (ZvT)

Strengths

  • Excellent vs Marine/Medivac
  • Muta harassment hits mineral lines
  • Baneling bombs vs Marine clumps
  • Map control with speed

Watch For

  • Thors, Tanks, mass Turrets
  • Widow Mine fields
  • Viking responses to Mutalisks
  • Mech transitions

vs Zerg (ZvZ)

Key Elements

  • Ling/Bane wars require superior micro
  • Muta vs Muta numbers crucial
  • Speed advantage often decisive
  • Baneling connections game-changing

Watch For

  • Roach transitions
  • Spore Crawler defense
  • Infestor responses
  • Economic builds to out-macro

Economic Management

Gas Usage

High Gas Demand

  • Banelings and Mutalisks are gas-heavy
  • Take 3 gas geysers minimum
  • Gas priority: Speed → Lair → Spire → Units
  • Balance mineral/gas spending

Base Management

  • Fast third for economy
  • 50-60 drones optimal
  • Critical inject timing
  • Essential creep spread

Advanced Micro Techniques

Mutalisk Micro

  • Magic Box: Spread Mutalisks to reduce splash
  • Stacking: Move as single unit for speed
  • Target Fire: Focus important targets
  • Clumping: Re-stack after spreading

Baneling Micro

  • Split Prevention: Force detonations on target
  • Flanking: Attack from unexpected angles
  • Escort: Use Zerglings to protect Banelings
  • Manual Detonation: Don't waste Banelings

Multi-tasking

  • Dual Control: Micro both air and ground armies
  • Harassment: Mutalisks while ground army fights
  • Map Awareness: Track multiple enemy forces
  • Production: Keep building units during micro

Common Mistakes

  • Poor Mutalisk micro vs anti-air
  • Clumping Mutalisks against splash damage
  • Wasting Banelings on wrong targets
  • Not multitasking effectively
  • Over-investing in air without ground support
  • Fighting in bad positions
  • Not spreading creep for mobility
  • Missing inject timings during micro

Upgrade Priority

  1. Metabolic Boost: Essential Zergling speed
  2. Centrifugal Hooks: Baneling speed
  3. Flyer Attacks +1: Mutalisk damage
  4. Melee Attacks +1: Zergling/Baneling damage
  5. Flyer Carapace: Mutalisk survivability

Defending Against Counters

vs Mass Anti-Air

Adaptations

  • Reduce Mutalisk count
  • Focus on ground army
  • Hit and run tactics only
  • Consider tech transitions

vs Splash Damage

  • Spread units effectively
  • Attack from multiple angles
  • Use mobility to avoid damage
  • Fight on favorable terms

Late Game Transitions

Tech Additions

  • Add Ultralisks: For late-game power
  • Hive Tech: Greater Spire for Brood Lords
  • Infestors: Fungal Growth support
  • Economic: Take more bases for sustainability

Map Control Strategy

  • Watchtowers: Control key vision points
  • Creep Highways: Fast movement corridors
  • Overlord Spread: Map-wide vision
  • Denial: Prevent enemy expansion
  • Retreat Paths: Always have escape routes

Practice Drills

  • Practice Mutalisk magic box micro
  • Work on Baneling connection accuracy
  • Practice multitasking air and ground
  • Learn optimal unit ratios
  • Practice creep spread patterns
  • Work on inject timing during battles
