Roach/Hydra Composition

Matchup: ZvP, ZvT

Difficulty: Intermediate

A powerful mid-to-late game army composition combining the tankiness of Roaches with the range and DPS of Hydralisks. This build excels against Protoss death balls and Terran mech compositions.

Back to Zerg Builds

Build Order

  1. 9 Overlord
  2. 15 Hatchery
  3. 16 Spawning Pool
  4. 17 Extractor
  5. 18 Overlord
  6. @Pool: Queen, 4 Zerglings
  7. 22 Roach Warren
  8. @Natural: Queen
  9. 26 Lair
  10. 28 Extractor x2
  11. @Lair: Hydralisk Den
  12. 32 Evolution Chamber x2
  13. @Den: Hydralisk production
  14. Continue Roach/Hydra production
  15. Add Infestation Pit for Hive tech

Unit Composition

Army Ratios

  • Roaches: 40-50% (front line tanks)
  • Hydralisks: 50-60% (DPS and anti-air)
  • Support Units: Overseers, Queens, Infestors

Production Balance

  • Early: Roach-heavy for defense
  • Mid: Balanced Roach/Hydra
  • Late: Hydra-heavy with Roach support

Execution Guide

Early Game (0-8 minutes)

  • Standard economic opening
  • Get Roach Warren for early defense
  • Scout opponent's tech choices
  • Defend with Roaches if pressured
  • Focus on economic development

Tech Phase (8-12 minutes)

  • Get Lair as soon as economically safe
  • Add Hydralisk Den when Lair completes
  • Start Evolution Chambers for upgrades
  • Begin Hydralisk production
  • Take third base

Army Phase (12+ minutes)

  • Mass produce Roach/Hydra army
  • Get critical upgrades (+1/+1 minimum)
  • Control key map positions
  • Force favorable engagements
  • Tech to Hive for advanced units

Key Strategic Points

  • Range Advantage: Hydralisks outrange most units
  • Anti-Air: Strong against air compositions
  • Tanky Front-line: Roaches absorb damage
  • Upgrade Dependent: Scales excellently with upgrades
  • Positioning Critical: Army must be well-positioned

Unit Roles and Usage

Roach Role

  • Tank: Absorb damage in front
  • Anti-Armor: Good vs armored units
  • Regeneration: Heal when out of combat
  • Mobility: Fast movement for repositioning

Hydralisk Role

  • DPS: Primary damage dealers
  • Anti-Air: Counter air units
  • Range: Attack from safe distance
  • Focus Fire: Eliminate key targets

Positioning and Formation

Army Formation

  • Roaches Front: Tank damage in front line
  • Hydras Back: DPS from behind Roaches
  • Concave: Spread army in arc formation
  • High Ground: Use terrain advantages

Engagement Positioning

  • Range Advantage: Start fights at max Hydra range
  • Retreat Path: Always have escape route
  • Support Units: Keep Overseers/Queens safe
  • Flanking: Attack from multiple angles when possible

Matchup Specific Strategy

vs Protoss (ZvP)

  • Excellent Against: Gateway units, Colossi
  • Target Priority: Colossi > Stalkers > Zealots
  • Watch For: High Templar, Archons, Void Rays
  • Upgrades: Range upgrade crucial vs Colossi
  • Support: Add Vipers for Blinding Cloud

vs Terran (ZvT)

  • Excellent Against: Mech compositions, Battlecruisers
  • Target Priority: Tanks > Thors > other units
  • Watch For: Bio compositions, Liberators
  • Positioning: Avoid Tank lines, use mobility
  • Support: Add Vipers vs mech, Infestors vs bio

Economic Management

Gas Requirements

  • High Gas Demand: Both units require gas
  • Gas Timing: Take all geysers by 8 minutes
  • Gas Priority: Den > Units > Upgrades
  • Saturation: 6 drones on gas minimum

Base Management

  • Three Base Economy: Needed for gas-heavy composition
  • Drone Count: 60-70 drones optimal
  • Inject Timing: Critical for unit production
  • Expansion Timing: Third base around 8-10 minutes

Upgrade Priority

Essential Upgrades

  1. Grooved Spines: +1 Hydralisk range
  2. Missile Attacks +1: Roach and Hydra damage
  3. Carapace +1: Survivability for both units
  4. Muscular Augments: Hydralisk speed
  5. Tunneling Claws: Roach speed

Upgrade Timing

  • Range First: Get Grooved Spines immediately
  • Double Upgrades: Research attack and armor together
  • Speed Upgrades: After combat upgrades
  • Continuous: Always be upgrading something

Micro Techniques

Hydralisk Micro

  • Focus Fire: Target single enemies
  • Kiting: Use range to avoid damage
  • Spreading: Avoid splash damage
  • Target Priority: Focus threats first

Roach Micro

  • Tank Damage: Keep in front of Hydras
  • Regeneration: Pull back damaged Roaches
  • Flanking: Use speed for positioning
  • Target Selection: Focus armored units

Army Control

  • Formation: Maintain proper unit spacing
  • Retreat: Pull back as single army
  • Reinforcement: Rally new units to army
  • Positioning: Always fight from good positions

Common Mistakes

  • Fighting without range upgrade vs Colossi
  • Poor army positioning and formation
  • Not getting enough gas for composition
  • Engaging in bad positions
  • Neglecting upgrades for more units
  • Clumping army against splash damage
  • Not using Roaches as proper tanks

Advanced Techniques

Army Splitting

  • Multi-pronged Attacks: Attack from multiple angles
  • Flanking: Surround enemy army
  • Base Harassment: Small groups hit expansions
  • Retreat Splitting: Save units during retreat

Support Unit Integration

  • Overseers: Detection and vision
  • Infestors: Fungal Growth for crowd control
  • Vipers: Blinding Cloud vs ranged units
  • Queens: Transfuse for healing

Defending With Roach/Hydra

Defensive Positioning

  • Choke Points: Use terrain advantages
  • High Ground: Fight from elevated positions
  • Spines Support: Spine Crawlers for extra DPS
  • Queen Support: Transfuse and anti-air

Counter-Attack Timing

  • After Defense: Counter when enemy retreats
  • Multi-tasking: Hit enemy base while they attack
  • Economic Damage: Target worker lines

Late Game Transitions

Hive Tech Additions

  • Lurkers: Add for area denial
  • Vipers: Support vs armored units
  • Brood Lords: Transition vs ground armies
  • Ultralisks: Add vs light units

Composition Evolution

  • Early: Pure Roach/Hydra
  • Mid: Add support units
  • Late: Incorporate Hive tech
  • Max Supply: Optimal unit mix

Dealing with Counters

vs High Templar

  • Spread Army: Reduce Storm damage
  • Focus Fire: Kill Templars first
  • Flanking: Attack from multiple sides
  • Support: Add Vipers for Abduct

vs Siege Tanks

  • Mobility: Use speed to avoid Tank lines
  • Multi-angle: Attack from different directions
  • Support: Vipers for Blinding Cloud
  • Focus Fire: Eliminate Tanks quickly

vs Air Units

  • Hydra Focus: More Hydralisks vs air
  • Queen Support: Additional anti-air
  • Spore Crawlers: Static defense
  • Corruptors: Dedicated anti-air units

Map Control Strategy

  • Vision: Overlord spread for map awareness
  • Creep: Connect bases with creep highways
  • Watchtowers: Control key vision points
  • Expansion Denial: Prevent enemy expansions
  • Retreat Paths: Always have escape routes

Production Management

Building Requirements

  • Roach Warren: 1 per 2 bases minimum
  • Hydralisk Den: 1-2 depending on composition
  • Evolution Chambers: 2 for double upgrades
  • Hatcheries: 3-4 for proper production

Production Cycles

  • Larvae Management: Always inject on time
  • Unit Queue: Continuous production
  • Supply Management: Stay ahead on Overlords
  • Resource Balance: Match gas income to production

Practice Drills

  • Practice army positioning and formation
  • Work on Hydralisk focus fire micro
  • Practice retreat and re-engage timing
  • Learn optimal upgrade timings
  • Practice transitioning to Hive tech

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Excellent vs gateway units and Colossi
  • Strong anti-air capabilities
  • Good range and DPS
  • Scales well with upgrades
  • Flexible composition

Weaknesses

  • Vulnerable to AoE damage
  • Slow army movement
  • High gas requirements
  • Positioning dependent
  • Weak vs Storm and Siege Tanks
