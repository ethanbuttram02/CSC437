Fast 3rd CC

Matchup: TvZ (primarily)

Difficulty: Intermediate-Advanced

This build is great against the TvZ matchup because the Zerg opponent will almost always be out-mining you. This economic strategy focuses on matching Zerg's expansion speed to compete in the late game.

  12. 32 Bunker (if needed vs pressure)
  13. @Factory: Hellions or Widow Mines
  14. @Starport: Medivacs or Vikings
  15. Continue economic development

Execution Guide

Early Game (0-6 minutes)

  • Execute standard Terran opening
  • Take natural expansion at 21 supply
  • Build Factory for tech structure
  • Scout Zerg's build order and aggression timing
  • Focus on SCV production over military

Economic Phase (6-10 minutes)

  • Take third Command Center early (around 6-7 minutes)
  • Build minimal defensive units (Hellions/Marines)
  • Get Starport for mobility/detection
  • Scout heavily for Zerg timing attacks
  • Prepare defensive measures if needed

Transition Phase (10+ minutes)

  • Saturate all three bases with SCVs
  • Add production facilities (Barracks/Factories)
  • Choose army composition based on Zerg's strategy
  • Get upgrades and tech up
  • Maintain economic advantage

Key Strategic Points

  • Economic Focus: Prioritize economy over early military
  • Scouting Critical: Must know when Zerg attacks
  • Defensive Preparation: Ready to defend timing attacks
  • Late Game Strength: Superior economy for extended games
  • Risk vs Reward: Vulnerable early, powerful late

Economic Management

SCV Production

  • Constant Production: Never stop making SCVs
  • Target Count: 70+ SCVs across three bases
  • MULE Usage: Drop MULEs on farthest minerals
  • Saturation: 16 SCVs per mineral line + gas workers

Base Timing

  • Natural: Around 3:30-4:00
  • Third: Around 6:30-7:00
  • Fourth: 12-15 minutes if game continues
  • Orbital Timing: Get Orbitals as soon as CCs complete

Scouting and Information

Critical Scouting Points

  • Zerg's Opening: Hatch first vs Pool first
  • Timing Attacks: Roach rushes, Ling/Bane all-ins
  • Tech Choices: Spire, Hydra Den, Infestation Pit
  • Base Count: How many bases Zerg is taking
  • Army Composition: What units Zerg is building

Scouting Methods

  • SCV Scout: Early game base check
  • Hellions: Mid-game harassment and scouting
  • Medivacs: Air scouting and drops
  • Scans: Orbital Command energy for vision
  • Vikings: Air superiority and vision

Defending Against Zerg Aggression

Early Aggression (6-8 minutes)

  • Speedling Floods: Wall off, bunkers, Marines
  • Roach Rushes: Marauders, bunkers, repair SCVs
  • Baneling Busts: Spread buildings, Marine splits
  • General Rule: Don't over-react to small attacks

Mid-Game Timing (10-12 minutes)

  • Ling/Bane/Muta: Turrets, Marines, Tank support
  • Roach/Hydra: Tank lines, Marine/Marauder
  • Two-Base All-ins: Full defensive setup
  • Positioning: Defend at choke points

Army Composition Choices

Bio Transition

  • Units: Marine/Marauder/Medivac
  • Advantages: Mobile, good harassment
  • Add-ons: Mines, Liberators, Ghosts
  • Best vs: Ling/Bane/Muta compositions

Mech Transition

  • Units: Tank/Hellbat/Thor
  • Advantages: Strong late game, cost effective
  • Add-ons: Vikings, Ravens
  • Best vs: Ground-heavy Zerg armies

Hybrid Compositions

  • Bio + Tanks: Mobile with siege support
  • Mech + Bio: Tanks with Marine support
  • Sky Terran: Battlecruiser/Viking/Raven

Upgrade Priorities

Economic Upgrades

  • Orbital Commands: Essential for MULE income
  • Engineering Bays: For upgrades and Turrets
  • Planetary Fortress: Defensive upgrade for third

Military Upgrades

  • Infantry/Vehicle Weapons: Damage increases
  • Infantry/Vehicle Plating: Survivability
  • Stim Pack: If going bio
  • Siege Mode: If going mech

Common Mistakes

  • Not scouting for Zerg timing attacks
  • Over-investing in defense against small pressure
  • Taking third base too early vs aggressive Zerg
  • Not building enough production facilities
  • Forgetting to get military tech structures
  • Poor SCV rallying to distant bases
  • Not adapting army composition to Zerg's units

Advanced Techniques

Economic Optimization

  • MULE Stacking: Multiple MULEs on rich minerals
  • Base Preparation: Pre-build CCs for faster expansion
  • SCV Rallying: Efficient worker distribution
  • Supply Management: Stay ahead on supply

Defensive Positioning

  • Choke Control: Defend at narrow points
  • High Ground: Use terrain advantages
  • Overlapping Fields: Mutual building support
  • Repair Support: SCV micro during battles

Map Considerations

Good Maps for Fast 3CC

  • Large macro maps with safe third bases
  • Maps with defensible choke points
  • High ground advantages near expansions
  • Multiple expansion options

Difficult Maps

  • Small rush distance maps
  • Open third base locations
  • Maps with multiple attack paths
  • Limited defensive positions

Matchup Specific Notes

vs Standard Zerg

  • Safe Build: Standard economic Zerg builds
  • Timing: Take third around 7 minutes
  • Scouting: Regular check-ins with Hellions
  • Transition: Match Zerg's tech choices

vs Aggressive Zerg

  • Risky Build: Against timing attack builds
  • Timing: Delay third if pressure detected
  • Defense: More military, fewer SCVs temporarily
  • Adaptation: May need to abandon third temporarily

Late Game Advantages

  • Economic Superior: More income than Zerg
  • Tech Access: Can afford advanced units
  • Upgrade Advantage: Better upgrade economy
  • Multiple Fronts: Can sustain longer battles
  • Base Trading: Can out-economy Zerg

Transition Timings

Military Transition (10-12 minutes)

  • Add 3-5 production buildings
  • Choose army composition
  • Begin upgrade research
  • Start applying pressure

Late Game (15+ minutes)

  • Advanced tech units (Ghosts, Ravens, BCs)
  • Multiple upgrade paths
  • Fourth base consideration
  • Map control and harassment

Practice Drills

  • Practice build order vs AI
  • Work on SCV production efficiency
  • Practice defending common Zerg timings
  • Learn optimal MULE placement
  • Practice smooth transitions to military
