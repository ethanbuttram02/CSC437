Proxy Stargate

Matchup: All matchups

Difficulty: Intermediate

An aggressive build that places a Stargate close to the opponent's base for fast air unit harassment. This strategy relies on surprise and can deal significant economic damage or end games quickly.

Execution Guide

Opening (0-4 minutes)

  • Execute standard gateway opening
  • Scout opponent's base layout
  • Send Probe to proxy location around 3:30
  • Hide Probe until ready to build

Proxy Phase (4-6 minutes)

  • Build Stargate in hidden location
  • Keep Probe alive and hidden
  • Start first air unit immediately
  • Prepare follow-up units

Attack Phase (6+ minutes)

  • Attack with first air unit
  • Target workers and key buildings
  • Continuously produce air units
  • Support with gateway units if needed

Proxy Locations

Ideal Positions

  • Dead Space: Areas opponent rarely scouts
  • Behind Minerals: Hidden from main base vision
  • High Ground: Harder to spot from below
  • Corner Maps: Edges of the map
  • Near Natural: Close to secondary target

What to Avoid

  • Obvious scouting paths
  • Areas with enemy unit patrol
  • Too close to enemy anti-air
  • Locations with no escape route
  • Areas visible from main base

Unit Choice and Usage

Phoenix

Best vs: Terran, Protoss

  • Advantages: Fast, mobile, good vs light units
  • Targets: Workers, Marines, Stalkers
  • Abilities: Graviton Beam for lifting units
  • Micro: Hit and run, lift key units

Void Ray

Best vs: Zerg, Protoss

  • Advantages: Strong vs buildings, scales up
  • Targets: Buildings, heavy units
  • Abilities: Charge beam for increased damage
  • Micro: Focus firing, beam management

Oracle

Best vs: All matchups

  • Advantages: Worker harassment, revelation
  • Targets: Workers, key tech buildings
  • Abilities: Pulsar Beam, Revelation
  • Micro: Worker line harassment

Key Strategic Points

  • Surprise Factor: Proxy must stay hidden
  • Economic Damage: Target workers first
  • Timing: Hit before anti-air is ready
  • Follow-up: Continue pressure or transition
  • Probe Management: Keep proxy Probe alive

Matchup Specific Strategy

vs Terran

  • Unit Choice: Phoenix preferred
  • Targets: SCVs, Marines, Medivacs
  • Watch For: Marines, Turrets, Vikings
  • Timing: Before significant Marine count
  • Follow-up: Gateway units or expand

vs Protoss

  • Unit Choice: Phoenix or Void Ray
  • Targets: Probes, Pylons, tech buildings
  • Watch For: Stalkers, Photon Cannons
  • Timing: Before Stalker mass or air response
  • Follow-up: Tech transition or all-in

vs Zerg

  • Unit Choice: Void Ray or Oracle
  • Targets: Drones, Queens, Overlords
  • Watch For: Queens, Spore Crawlers, Hydras
  • Timing: Before Spire or Hydra Den
  • Follow-up: Pressure or macro behind

Common Mistakes

  • Proxy Stargate spotted by enemy scout
  • Poor proxy location choice
  • Not bringing enough air units
  • Attacking into prepared anti-air
  • Losing the proxy Probe too early
  • No follow-up plan if harassment fails
  • Over-committing against heavy defense
  • Not scouting for enemy tech responses

Advanced Techniques

  • Double Proxy: Two Stargates for more units
  • Fake Retreat: Bait out anti-air, then re-engage
  • Multi-angle Attack: Hit from different directions
  • Probe Micro: Use proxy Probe for scouting
  • Pylon Power: Build Pylons to power proxy

Micro Techniques

Phoenix Micro

  • Graviton Beam: Lift key units (Tanks, Queens)
  • Hit and Run: Attack and retreat before taking damage
  • Worker Harassment: Focus on mineral line raids
  • Range Kiting: Use 4 range to stay safe

Void Ray Micro

  • Beam Focus: Charge up on buildings or units
  • Target Selection: Focus important structures
  • Positioning: Stay at max range when possible
  • Retreat Timing: Leave before taking heavy damage

Scouting and Information

Pre-Proxy Scouting

  • Check opponent's build order
  • Look for early anti-air preparation
  • Identify best proxy locations
  • Note enemy scouting patterns

During Attack Scouting

  • Check for defensive buildings
  • Monitor enemy unit production
  • Watch for counter-attacks
  • Assess damage dealt

Defending Against Proxy Stargate

  • Scout Early: Check for proxy buildings
  • Build Detection: Observers, Overseers, Scans
  • Anti-Air Units: Marines, Stalkers, Queens
  • Static Defense: Turrets, Cannons, Spores
  • Counter-Attack: Hit Protoss while they're committed

Transition Options

If Harassment Succeeds

  • Continue air unit production
  • Add more Stargates
  • Expand behind the pressure
  • Transition to normal tech

If Harassment Fails

  • Recall air units to defend
  • Focus on gateway units
  • Defensive play until caught up
  • Consider different tech path

Economic Considerations

  • Investment: Stargate + air units is expensive
  • Pay-off: Must deal significant damage
  • Risk: Can fall behind if unsuccessful
  • Timing: Earlier attack = higher chance of success

Map Considerations

Good Maps for Proxy Stargate

  • Large maps with many hiding spots
  • Maps with dead space areas
  • High ground positions near opponent
  • Maps with limited scouting paths

Difficult Maps

  • Small maps with limited hiding spots
  • Open maps with clear sightlines
  • Maps with easy defensive positions
  • Rush distance maps

Practice Drills

  • Practice hiding proxy buildings on different maps
  • Work on air unit micro vs anti-air
  • Practice worker harassment patterns
  • Learn optimal proxy timing
  • Practice transitioning after harassment
